Planets in Solar System

Our Solar System is comprised of nine planets. The listed-sequential order of the planets from the sun is the following: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and the dwarf planet Pluto.

Discovery of Planets

From the Earth, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, and Saturn can be seen with the naked eye; thus, the time of their discovery is unknown. However, Uranus was discovered by William Hershel on March 13, 1781; Neptune was discovered by Johann Galle and Heinrich Louis d’Arrest, with the use of LaVerrier’s position, on September 18, 1846; and Pluto was discovered by Clyde Tombaugh on February 18, 1930.
